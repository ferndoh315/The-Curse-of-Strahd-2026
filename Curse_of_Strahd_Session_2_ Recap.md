# 🩸 Curse of Strahd — Session 2 Recap
*The Road to Barovia · The Durst Manor ·*

---

## 🔥 Chapter I: Breaking Free

The session picked up exactly where the last one ended. The party was frozen in place as two Red Wizards shouted over each other in the burning Clockwork Stein about the ominous voice’s interruption. "The patron of this... the source of this ice slaughter... has betrayed us," one of the Red Wizards snapped. Their argument cost them their concentration.

**Chuck** felt the paralysis loosen first. He wasted no time: two magic missiles into the bald wizard who'd slaughtered the vampiric goliaths, a third into the other, drawing blood and drawing attention. Then, rather than escalating, he deployed a cascade of blinding sparkles between them with prestidigitation. Not damaging. Just infuriating. It worked. The wizards cut their losses and fled, dropping their spell entirely.

The party crashed to the floor, gasping and suddenly free. The building was still on fire.

The only exit was the stage door. They ran. A collapsing pillar crushed the stage behind them. In the chaos, **Seren** was struck and thrown back into the fire, separated from the group entirely. The others had no time to stop. The vargheist creatures, those same bat-winged horrors from earlier in the night, poured out of the smoke behind the fleeing party. The ground cracked open as Neverwinter’s underground magma produced fissures along the city’s northern edge, sending snow into instant fog. The group burst through a gap in the crumbling outer wall and into the woods beyond, chased by monsters and swallowed by mist.

The sounds of the city died. The cold felt different. Wrong.

*What became of Seren is unknown for now. Her story will be told.*

---

## 🌫️ Chapter II: A Road That Wasn't There Before

Darkvision couldn't cut the fog. Survival checks failed. **Mumbles** deployed his clockwork firefly-lanterns, the only light for miles. The group pushed forward despite not knowing which direction forward was.

Then the fog thinned just enough to show a winding muddy road. And beyond it: a castle on a distant crag that none of them recognized.

It was here, slipping through the mist on that unknown road, that the party formally introduced themselves for the first time. Names, rough histories, the shared absurdity of their situation. **Loma** scanned the sky and found no stars she recognized. **Mumbles'** menagerie of clocks were all spinning randomly — though he introduced himself to the group under a different name. The party was none the wiser, for now, that the name they were given was not the whole truth of who he was. The climate was wrong. Everything was wrong.

They were no longer on the Sword Coast.

---

## 👴 Chapter III: The Old Man on the Rock

A figure sat on a stone beside the road, hunched and patient. He had been waiting for precisely this group, at precisely this time. He introduced himself as **Velasi von Holtz**, unbothered by the fog or the castle on the hill, which told them something about how long he had been in this land.

He had been paid to wait by a patron he did not name, to give things to people he had not met but apparently recognized on sight. He handed over a map and a sealed letter. When pressed on where they were, he answered plainly: **Barovia**. He had not heard of Neverwinter. He directed them toward the village of Barovia, just past an abandoned town, and wished them luck. He began to walk away mostly ignoring the group’s bombardment of questions. 

The letter bore a wax seal and inside, an invitation to dinner at a castle. The host claimed to have brought them here himself. He promised safe passage. He signed it:

> *"Your host, **Strahd von Zarovich**."*

None of them knew the name. They would soon meet someone who did.

---

## ⚔️ Chapter IV: The Man in the Road

*"Get off me, you filthy dogs."*

Around a bend: a lone fighter in battered armor, surrounded by a pack of scrawny werewolves, his greatsword already moving. He was holding his own. The party helped anyway.

**Malor** struck first. A greatsword blow connected cleanly and erupted in holy light as divine smite cracked through the blade like thunder. **Loma** followed, pressing in from stealth with her blades. **Qyleth** came next, reaching out with a chill touch that withered one of the remaining creatures from the tail upward. Seeing Loma hold her own in the thick of it, Qyleth turned to her with a word of genuine praise and passed her a bardic inspiration. **Lumi** pounced and pinned. **Mumbles** released fire ants. The pack broke and fled.

**Malor** was from Barovia. Born here. Had never heard of Neverwinter, the Sword Coast, or anything the group said was beyond the mists. When the letter was read aloud and **Strahd's** name was spoken, his expression shifted.

*Everyone in Barovia knows that name. The wise ones accept his invitations.*

He agreed to travel with them.

---

## 👧 Chapter V: The Children in the Mist

Near the abandoned town: the sound of children arguing. **Rose** and **Thorn** appeared from the dark, a girl keeping her composure and a younger boy clutching a stuffed toy. Their story came in pieces. A monster in the basement. Baby brother **Walter** still inside. Parents fighting it. Can't go in alone. Father has gold.

That last part landed. The party followed the children to the manor.

On a perimeter check, **Loma** found the family mausoleum in the garden. Spaces for two new residents were being prepared. The nameplates: **Rose** and **Thorn**.

The children who led them here were almost certainly already dead.

---

## 🏚️ Chapter VI: Death House

The Durst Manor was once magnificent: mahogany, crystal, red velvet, family portraits, a golden windmill crest in the foyer. Suits of armor flanking the mantel. From the moment the door opened, faint crying could be heard filtering down from somewhere above. A baby, by the sound of it. Nobody said it out loud, but everyone heard it.

**First floor:** The party spread through the ground level. **Qyleth** slipped into the dining room and, on a hunch, ran her hand under the table. Tucked in a hidden compartment: an obsidian dagger, its blade carved with strange markings and emitting a faint, low hum. She pocketed it without ceremony.

**Second floor:** Durst Manor looked and felt more abandoned with each floor. The study consumed most of their attention. **Mumbles** and **Loma** turned the desk upside down and a key was found in a drawer's hidden compartment. **Malor** shook books off shelves until one refused to move. A lever behind it swung open a hidden door into a cramped ritual room: occult circles on the floor, flesh-bound tomes lining the walls, and a chest with a dart-riddled skeleton draped over it. Loma identified the pressure plates and dart mechanisms before anyone triggered them fatally. Mumbles retrieved the contents: a coin bearing **Strahd's** face and a letter from Strahd *to the Durst family*, contemptuous and dismissive. Their sacrifices, their dungeon victims, their blood offerings: none of it had summoned him. They were, he wrote, *"worms writhing in my earth."* **Chuck** was able to discern all of this "occult activity" was the work of charlatans. The "cult" at the Durst Manor was dealing with forces they had no idea how to leverage or work with.  

Elsewhere on the same floor, a room held a harpsichord and a piano. **Chuck** sat down and began to play. The crying from above, which had been building since they entered, softened and went quiet. He moved to the piano. Same result. Something upstairs was listening.

**Third floor:** Not everyone went up. **Mumbles** and **Chuck**, having weighed their options yet again, decided the third floor could wait and slipped back down to the kitchen instead. The ground floor larder looked promising: salted meats hanging from the ceiling, charcuterie boards, food that appeared, against all odds in this decaying house, fresh and good. They helped themselves. Chuck even magically smoked some of the meat. It tasted delicious.

Then **Chuck** bit into a sausage and found a fingernail.

They looked at each other. They looked at the kitchen. They looked at the rest of the meat. A silent agreement was reached: nobody upstairs needed to know about this right now. The Durst family, it turned out, had not been buying their meat from any butcher. They set down their food and said nothing further on the matter.

Meanwhile, upstairs, an **animated suit of armor** attacked without warning, having already crushed **Mumbles'** scout-bug when it scouted ahead. **Malor** absorbed a shield-bash and smited through the return swing. **Loma's** blades bloodied the horror with a crit and kept it pressed. **Lumi** snared the creature with her powerful jaws. The armor finally folded into a pile of metal and silence. What waited beyond it was quieter and stranger.

Making use of the key from the office desk, they entered the nursery. The source of the crying. Inside, a spectral nursemaid paced beside a crib, cooing to what looked like a well-swaddled infant that held only a toy and a pendant. It was only a bundle of blankets upon further inspection. She spoke as though the household were still alive. After some questioning, she indicated a hidden staircase behind a bookshelf through helpful gestures. The servants’ passages. Her ephemeral hands passed through the lever to gain entry. **Malor's** sword did not. She then reappeared where the party had originally found her. Cooing at the crib to soothe the "child" again. 

**The Attic:** Two child-sized skeletons in the corner of a bricked-up room, dressed exactly as **Rose** and **Thorn**. The younger one clutched a doll identical to Thorn's. The ghosts of both children appeared, cheerful and unaware, asking if everyone had come to play. They were excited to meet the party for the first time...

## ⚰️ Chapter VII: What the Crypt Gave Back

On the way out, **Loma** paused at the nursemaid's crib. The spectral woman was still looping, still cooing to nothing. Inside the bundle she had been cradling all along: a small pendant and a rattle, the only real things in her arms. Loma took them quietly and said nothing.

The bones of **Rose** and **Thorn** were carried to the mausoleum and interred properly. As Rose's remains slid into place, the stone slab caught on something. A small coffer, hidden behind the slab. Inside: scattered coins, toys, a child's secret treasury.

Before any reasonable discussion could happen, the coins were already being divided between **Malor** and mostly **Mumbles**. **Loma** wasn’t having it. She called it out directly: “You would not even have come if you hadn't been pushed to!” The argument’s resolution will have to be the first order of business come next session.

**Items recovered this session:**
> 🗡️ Obsidian ritual dagger, faint arcane humming (dining table hidden compartment, first floor)
> 🪙 Coin bearing Strahd's face (cultist chest, second floor)
> 📜 Letter from Strahd to the Durst family (cultist chest, second floor)
> 💰 Gold coins from Rose's hidden coffer (disputed, unresolved)

As the mausoleum was sealed, chanting drifted up from somewhere below the house: rhythmic, reverent, repeating the name *von Zarovich*. The basement of Durst Manor was not explored. Whatever the family was doing down there is unfinished.

The party chose the road.
