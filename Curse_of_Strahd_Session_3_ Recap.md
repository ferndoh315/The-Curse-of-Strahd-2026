# 🩸 Curse of Strahd — Session 3 Recap
*The Village of Barovia · Van Richten's Study · The Ravens Reliquary · The Blood on the Vine*

---

## 🌙 Interlude: Seren's Solo Adventure

While the rest of the party fled through the mist, **Seren Vael** woke face-up in the burning wreckage of the Clockwork Stein, her astrolabe spinning wildly above her chest. She was not alone.

A void-like entity loomed over her: A black miasma in human form with a swirling center mass of darkness, like a black hole with stars trapped inside it. It reached toward her. **Seren** called down a pillar of sacred flame between them. The entity raised one hand and simply absorbed it, extinguishing the divine fire without effort. Then it spoke:

> *"Why are you here? You should have left with them."*

Before it could reach her, a hand grabbed her from behind. "Have faith in The Moonmaiden and The Morninglord." A knight in armor adorned with white robes marked with red crosses hauled her onto his shoulder and burst through a window. The sword in his other hand was made of clear diamond or amethyst-looking material and threaded with iridescent cracks. And when he leveled it toward the entity, the light that poured from it made the void-thing recoil, slow, and begin to disintegrate at its edges.

His name was **Michael**, a Knight of the Holy Cross. The Morninglord, Lathander, had sent him. He told her to decide quickly. "I might be able to lead you to answers, but the window is closing," Michael stated amidst the chaos. **Seren** followed. Together they fled Neverwinter's seismic activity. The fissures caused a rapid melting of the surrounding snow and created a thick mist. But into the mist they went. The climate was quickly shifting from bitter cold to something more temperate as the city fell away. Seren's astrolabe gradually slowed into a curious pendulum swing angled up. When she looked up, the stars were in the wrong places: familiar in brightness, scrambled in position. A different sky entirely.

They found **Vasili von Holtz** waiting on his rock with one letter remaining. He expected HER, but not the knight. **Michael** cut the awkwardness and introduced himself. Vasili handed the letter over without ceremony and walked away. The letter was identical to the one the rest of the party carried. **Seren** and Michael continued to the village, passing through an abandoned town where children's laughter drifted out of the dark, and they stopped at the gates of Barovia to wait. Michael seemed entirely unsurprised by all of it.

## ⚔️ Chapter I: Reunited at the Gates

After **Loma** put **Dwight** on the spot over the split of the currency, the rest of the party emerged from the dilapidated village. As night deepened, the ghost children of **Rose** and **Thorn** waved cheerfully from the backyard of Durst Manor. They arrived at the gates of Barovia, flanked by two massive headless statues. There stood **Seren** beside a very large knight in impossibly clean white robes.

**Malor** was recognized at the gates by his fellow Barovians. The guards gestured about the rest of the party, asking if they should pass too. Malor gave a quick NO gesture, until **Chuck** quietly offered to cover his expenses. Gold is a universal language after all and Malor agreed. The letter these newcomers received matched the initial party's. Malor wanted to see where this would go. Finally something interesting in these dull lands. Chuck slipped the gold bribe to Malor. **Michael** passed all the scrutiny the party directed towards him with a calm demeanor. This guy's teeth even glowed faintly when he smiled. He was telling the truth. About everything. That made him stranger rather than more reassuring.

## 🎪 Chapter II: The Village of Barovia

The village was more alive than expected. A carnival occupied the town square, wine flowed freely from a visiting vintner, and the weekly finger puppet show was underway. The **Blood on the Vine Tavern**, which **Malor** had confidently named as the place to drink, was shuttered and dilapidated. He asked a local how long it had been closed. Months. Malor had only been gone a week. Nobody said anything about that.

The **New Blue Water Inn** was open and the free wine helped. The puppet show covered **Strahd's** greatest victories with infectious propaganda: the defeat of the Dusk Elves and their Sorcerer King **Kasimir**, a werewolf uprising led by an **Emil** was squashed, and the love story of Strahd and a woman named **Tatyana**. At the close of the performance, a shadowy figure on a fiery horse appeared in the mountains above the village, watching from a distance. Whether it was truly Strahd ensuring his story was told faithfully, nobody could say with certainty, though nobody doubted it either.

Then a red-robed lich erupted from the doors of the run-down Blood on the Vine Tavern. His hands spinning a massive fireball between them, cycling from orange to red to a blinding blue as it grew. The party recognized one of the four elegantly dressed, gaunt figures who came spilling out to contain it: **Elantris**, the woman who had arranged the Virellians' performance at the Clockwork Stein. Now clearly a vampire. They grilled the lich, who they called **Szass**, about running so far away from Thay. They knew he wanted something that was here. Something their lord had perhaps? Elantris and the other vampire nobles worked to hold back the blast until the lich swept their legs with a magical pass, broke their concentration, and sent the fireball detonating into the town square. People were incinerated where they stood. The party was thrown hard. **Loma** was knocked unconscious shielding **Lumi**, and **Mumbles** shocked her with a beetle to stabilize her. The party scattered, battered, and the vampires disappeared. Luckily, **Qyleth** caught where the nobles ran off to. Back into the Blood on the Vine. The lich vanished.

Then the church bells struck midnight. The hour of ghosts as the locals rightfully called it. From the ground, spectral figures began pulling themselves upward: adventurers of every kind in different states of violent ruin, forming a single-file line winding slowly up toward Castle Ravenloft. **Malor** knew this procession by heart. He explained it plainly: every night, replicas of anyone who had ever stood against **Strahd** were made to march to the castle. A reminder. The line grows longer and longer year after year... 

The party knocked on the Blood on the Vine's door. No answer. They decided to rest first.

As they tried to work out where to go, **Ezmerelda** appeared, moving through the ghostly procession and clearing a path through the specters. "Come with us," she stated. **Michael** stepped out alongside her and gestured the group forward. A door opened: an aging, slender and worn-looking man with a fully white head of hair, a ponytail and glasses appeared. **Rudolph Van Richten** was at the threshold, ushering them in. Michael's glowing touch ushered each member through the door, mending each member after the fireball.

## 🏺 Chapter III: The Monster Hunter

**Rudolph Van Richten** was not what anyone expected. His residence was a trophy hall for the impossible: manticore skulls, wyvern heads, a young blue dragon mounted on the wall, racks of flintlocks and crossbows, swords that looked like they had been pulled from other planes entirely. Taking up the remainder of his mantel: a single gargantuan arm covered in eyes from shoulder to fingertip. **Seren** identified it as a limb from the Hecatoncheires, a force of nature with fifty heads and a hundred arms, every one covered in eyes, standing a hundred stories tall. Even **Michael** went quiet at that.

**Van Richten** moved through his apothecary, tossing a scroll and a vial of anti-venom onto the table before introductions were finished. "You'll need this..." murmured the living legend. He read the letter from **Strahd**, confirmed its authenticity, and delivered his verdict: not ready. They pressed on this five times over and the answer was the same. When **Michael** asked what was different this time, Van Richten paused. Then: **Madam Eva had been taken**. That was what was different.

His protégé **Ezmerelda** was present throughout, though at one point an armored female behind a door called for Ez and she stepped away. 

The conversation that followed covered considerable ground. **Van Richten** explained the Vistani were the lore-keepers of this land and **Madam Eva** was their matriarch. The Vallaki Rebellion had fractured the land and driven many to the village of Barovia.

**Loma** then directly asked **Van Richten** what the Red Wizard cold-creature sacrifice rituals were really about, and for the first time Van Richten gave straight answers. The **Red Wizards**, led here by the lich **Szass Tam**, are trying to amplify their fire magic through cold-magic opposition. They are reaching out to drow across domains to help achieve it. Szass Tam had even abandoned his phylactery in another dimension entirely to be here. That told Van Richten something in Barovia was worth that risk.

**Van Richten** then turned his attention to **Qyleth**. He had noticed the signet ring. He asked about it directly. Qyleth explained: The ring was given to her about five or ten years ago by a cloaked stranger after a performance, and she was told it was *"for the last of them."* She had worn it ever since. Van Richten asked her to try to remove it. She pulled. The ring glowed red and refused to pass her first knuckle. Van Richten confirmed what she had never thought to check: she had never tried to take it off before. He couldn't place its origin, but told her plainly that she belonged to something — the ring came off no one's finger without their consent, and hers wouldn't come off at all. He was intrigued but disappointed in Qyleth for taking enchanted jewelry from strangers. 

His parting gifts: a **Wand of Wonders** to **Chuck**, seven uses, one per day, with the confidence of a man betting on someone he isn't sure will survive the week.

To **Malor**, who asked for something tangible, he offered advice instead: find a reason to live, not merely a reason to keep moving. Malor was unimpressed. 

Then a deck of cards appeared in **Qyleth's** pocket that she had not put there. **Van Richten** said *hit me* and nodded thoughtfully. After she dealt the card, Qyleth's ring glowed again. A regal spectral figure materialized, called Qyleth *sweet child* and murmured that its blood had gone far. The specter then retreated back into the ring.

**Mumbles**, on the way out, dropped some bugs on the floor. They crawled up toward a taxidermy **Gremishka** on **Van Richten's** mantel. The reanimated cat trophy began to clumsily walk, then shook violently and shot a scorching ray out of its mouth. Van Richten, without looking up, threw a counterspell vial at it. The cat survived. Mumbles owes him one. Rudolph let the reanimator artificer's new companion leave the shop. Maybe he could still use the space it would free up...

## 🌙 Chapter IV: The New Blue Water Inn

The party checked in for the night. The menu offered oats with local-fauna flavoring, honey at considerable expense, and wine in steady supply. **Qyleth** sat in the common room and played quietly in the corner while waiting for her glass, soft enough that it barely carried. The waiter bopped to it without noticing he was doing it. When she went upstairs, her linens were unusually warm and fresh. Someone on the staff had noticed after all.

**"Dwight"** — the name the party knows Mumbles by — was given a room with a crib in it. He complained immediately. The innkeeper, after some back-and-forth involving creative commentary about gnomish spatial requirements, agreed to bring in a proper bed. Dwight accepted this. He then lay down in the proper bed, found the blankets impossible to keep on in the cold, eyed the crib across the room, climbed in, burrowed himself into a burrito of blankets, and slept soundly and without further comment.

In the morning, the crib collapsed.

**Dwight** was trapped in the wreckage, tangled in the bars, and thoroughly unwilling to be seen that way. He heard footsteps in the hall — **Malor**, coming to check. Dwight yelled through the door that it was not Malor he wanted, it was **Chuck** or **Loma**, and absolutely not to come in. When Chuck arrived, the bed was on the floor, the crib was in pieces, and Dwight was in the middle of it all with the specific expression of someone who will deny this conversation later. Chuck helped him out. Dwight tried to mend the crib with magic. The Gremishka cat reacted with errant magic: a second mouth grew from the palm of Dwight's hand, and before he could do anything about it, the mouth lurched forward and expelled a hairball that shot sideways across the room and stuck to the wall. Barely missing Malor. If magic was cast near the Gremishka, it randomly expelled a spell of the same school. Dwight stared at his own hand in genuine terror. The mouth showed no signs of leaving. He went to breakfast with it still there, deeply unsettled, and it did not fully fade until the party crossed the threshold of the Ravens Reliquary later that morning. Nobody mentioned the crib.

**Michael** had already eaten and was on his second coffee. Patiently waiting. 

Morning in Barovia: overcast, always overcast, the sun a theoretical presence behind permanent cloud cover. The party could tell it was daytime because it was not pitch black.

## 🧭 Chapter V: Asking Around

Over the continental breakfast, the party tried to gather information about **Madam Eva**.

**Qyleth** tried the other patrons first. Her tiefling appearance drew open discomfort at the table, frightened a child, and produced nothing useful. She left.

**Loma** quietly asked the right people the right questions and came away with a direction: the trinket shop across the street, where the Vistani kept a presence. She passed the lead to the group.

On the way out, **Chuck** presented his wares of rusty daggers to a child and spun an elaborate tale of their legendary history. He charged three gold apiece. The child promptly picked his friend's pocket, bought the dagger, shanked his friend with it and ran. **Malor** magically provided some mending to the injured boy while searching both of the boy's pockets for his troubles. He pocketed some gold.

## 🔮 Chapter VI: The Ravens Reliquary

The shop was called the **Ravens Reliquary**: cluttered with curious objects, none obviously valuable, all somehow significant. Behind the counter stood **Ismark**, who greeted them warmly and became noticeably evasive the moment **Madam Eva's** name came up.

Outside the shop, peering through the window: a woman in armor that **Seren** recognized from **Van Richten's** back room — not **Ezmerelda**, but **Ireena**, the other woman who had briefly called her away. Seren flagged it to **Loma**. **Malor** decided this establishment was not for him and stayed outside. Loma went in and approached **Ismark** directly, leading the conversation. **Chuck** went to work on Ireena outside, drawing her attention away from whatever she had been searching for in the window.

**Qyleth** cast Detect Magic from outside the shop. The shelves lit up with signals across their length. **Ireena** registered nothing magical except for the sword at her hip, which glowed clearly. She carried no enchantments herself, only an enchanted weapon.

**Loma** pressed **Ismark** with gold until he cracked. What he told them: **Madam Eva** has visions whenever strangers arrive from distant lands, and she always feels compelled to help them begin their journey. The powers of Barovia had noticed the pattern. The last time anyone saw Madam Eva, she was in the company of four vampire nobles, being led away.

Outside, **Qyleth** had been pressing **Ireena** with her own questions. She brought up **Elantris** — the woman in red who had arranged the Virellians' performance at the Clockwork Stein, who she had since seen fighting to contain **Szass Tam's** fireball. She asked about her directly. Ireena's expression shifted. *That's the vampire lord's general of war,* she said. *What are you doing asking about her?* Qyleth absorbed this quietly. Elantris had booked a performance. Elantris had contained a fireball to protect the town. And Elantris was **Strahd's** general. The party had more questions now than when they walked in.

**"Dwight"** noticed a vial on the shelf unlike anything else in the shop: the size of a beaker, containing a black mist that moved constantly on its own while everything around it sat still. He pocketed it quietly while his Gremishka distracted the shopkeeper. The shopkeep none the wiser.

## 🩸 Chapter VII: Back to the Blood on the Vine

**Madam Eva**, last seen with four vampire nobles. **Qyleth** remembered: back in the town square, she had watched a group of four gaunt, elegantly dressed figures fighting to contain the lich's fireball before retreating into the dilapidated Blood on the Vine. Four of them. She told the group. They went back.

The doors were barred from the inside. **Malor** knocked. No answer. He put his shoulder into it and broke through.

Inside: black mist pooling on the floor, candles burned halfway down with blackened wicks, everything rummaged through and turned over. **Malor** knew this mist was a sign the vampire nobles were watching or in the area. He tried to abide by the established status quo. He asked where **Madam Eva** was. A reply was given in a misty whisper: Leave this place. He pressed for information again. In response, the mists swirled together and summoned a Gallows Speaker and two Mist Horrors.

The fight was chaotic and punishing. The mist horrors flickered between forms — faces, animal shapes, screaming black mist — moving erratically and striking hard. **Mumbles** opened with a crackling lightning strike that lit the first one up. **Qyleth** hurled Dissonant Whispers into the nearest horror, driving it backward, and used the opening to pass bardic inspiration to **Seren** and **Loma**. Seren cast Twilight Sanctuary, blooming a sphere of soft protective light over the party, and then Guiding Bolt cracked into one of the creatures with enough force to bloody it.

Then **Michael** said, "Shine, Amoracchius," as he drew Amoracchius slowly from its sheath and held it aloft. Blinding holy light poured from the blade's iridescent cracks. The remaining creatures writhed and recoiled, arms flying up to shield whatever passed for their faces. They condensed inward and fell prone. *"Join me,"* he said, looking at **Malor**. Then he slashed with enough might to cut the nearest creature in half if it had anything solid. With the creatures prone and helpless in the light, **Chuck** raised the Wand of Wonders. The Gallows Speaker was polymorphed into a frog. Malor walked over and stepped on it. That was that.

**Qyleth** moved in with the obsidian dagger she had found in the Durst Manor dining room, the one that had been humming since she pocketed it. She drove it into the prone creature's face. The moment the blade made contact, the humming became a pulse that radiated from the tip through the handle and up into her arm. She felt it: vitality transferred to her, healing and restoring her. The dagger wasn't just old. It was hungry.

**Chuck's** Sorcerous Burst twinned across both remaining creatures, vaporizing one. **Malor's** reluctant smite finished the Mist Horror. "A bit brutal in execution, but righteous nonetheless, brother," commented **Michael** to Malor. The room went still.

After all the creatures were banished, an immense force that felt like heavy gravity washed over the room and pushed on the party. It brought half of them to their knees as the others had to find something to brace themselves with. "What was that?!" **Qyleth** asked. "I believe that is my purpose for being here. And I welcome it," **Michael** replied. 

In the aftermath, the party searched the building. Someone handed **Qyleth** a book found in the sleeping quarters of the inn. With Comprehend Languages active, she pressed her hand to the page. The runes were not a written language at all, just symbols, and the pages shifted when she turned back to them. The spell was useless on them. She called out for anything else that needed reading. **Malor** handed over the inn's owner's journal. She put her hand to it and began reading aloud: names, dates, two dozen locals last seen being taken on particular days, and final entries describing the owner offering sacrifices and the bar's under-dwellings to **Strahd** in exchange for favor. The basement had been handed over freely.

**Seren** and **Michael** took a newly discovered star chart to the corner and spread it out. The notes described a sky that kept rearranging, and a swirling center of mass that bent light around it. They recognized it. They spoke quietly about what it might mean, and neither of them liked the answer. It reminded them of the creature they fled from at the Clockwork Stein. 

Behind the bar, the cash drawer concealed a safe combination written in three binding rune symbols. The safe itself was gone, just the runic combination under the till. And at the entrance to the basement: deep gouges in the doorframe at human-hand height, the marks of someone dragged through while fighting to hold on.

The party stood at the top and looked down.

_**Madam Eva** was last seen with four vampire nobles. The basement was given to **Strahd**. The dagger heals its wielder. The vial of black mist is in **Dwight's** pocket. The star chart confirms something **Seren** and **Michael** already feared. The stairs go down._
